Account Security

Q: How can I keep my account secure?

A: Don't give out your password to anyone at all for any reason
Not giving out your password is the most important step to keeping your account secure.
Only you have any need for your password. If another user asks you for your password, they are up to no good.
Toribash Community staff will never ask you for your password. Toribash Community staff have no use for your password. Do not give your password to anyone, even if they are staff.

It may seem silly, but most cases of account hijackings are the result of a user (knowingly or unknowingly) giving away their password. You are responsible for the activity on your account, so do not share your password with other people.

Be especially wary of websites offering to-good-to-be-true deals that require your Toribash password. Such websites are being used for phishing, and exist to steal your password. Never input your password on a website that doesn't end precisely with toribash.com.

Q: How do I make a good password?

A: Use a password manager.

A good password manager makes creating and using secure passwords very easy, and is recommended if you are concerned about the strength of your password. However, the bare minimum guidelines for a decent password are as follows:

  • Don't use the same password as you use on your email. This is very important -- don't let your email get stolen just because someone guessed your Toribash password.
  • Don't use the same password as you use on any other site, for the same reason.
  • Don't put your username in your password.
  • Don't put password in your password.
  • Don't use personally identifying information as your password, such as your birthday.
  • If you insist on memorizing your password, ensure that it is longer than 8 characters. Instead of trying to memorize a complicated sequence of symbols, it may be better to randomly pick 4 or 5 words from the dictionary, chain them together, and stick a random number somewhere in the middle.

Pick passwords that other people will be utterly incapable of guessing, and do not give your password to anyone, and you should be fine.

If you're using a manager that doesn't create passwords for you, at least use another form of strong password creation (i.e Strong Random Password Generator). It's usually unwise to try to make your own passwords!

Q: Anything else?

A: The final piece of the puzzle when it comes to account security is common sense.

  • Toribash staff will never, ever ask for your password.
  • You should never tell anybody your password under any circumstance.
  • Make sure you have a decent firewall/antivirus on your device. A keylogger will completely undo even the strongest passwords.
  • Only use your password to login to *.toribash.com, not t0ribash.org, tori.ru, toricredits.net or any other third-party site.
  • Finally, make sure your account uses a real email which you can access. If you ever need your password reset, this is an excellent method for us to verify who you are.
    • If you made your account ingame, you need to go to User Control Panel on the Forums to add an email to your account. It is very important that you do this so we can help you should something happen to your account.
