Taking Screenshots

Q: What are screenshots?

A: A screenshot is an image snapshot of your screen at the time it was taken. Often a screenshot will capture your entire screen, however Toribash come with built-in ways of exclusively capturing an image of the game itself.

Q: How do I take a screenshot in Toribash?

A: There are multiple commands to take a screenshot in Toribash:
F8, 0, /screenshot, and /ss
These commands will capture an image of the game at the time they are used. Nothing outside of the Toribash window will be captured.

Using PrtScn to make screenshots:

  • Capture a screen shot by hitting PrtScn on your keyboard. This will capture your entire screen and save it to your clipboard.
  • (Optional) Open an image editor (such as MS Paint). Paste your image (Ctrl+V on Windows, Command+V on Mac) into the editor and make any changes you need (make sure not to crop the Toribash window if you're using the screenshot as evidence in a report). When saving your image, make sure its saved as a .jpeg or .png, as other file extensions may interfere with the quality of the image.

Q: How do I share my screenshot?

A:

Finding your screenshot:

  • It's very easy to find screenshots. They will most likely be in the following directories on Local Disk (usually C:):
    • Standalone version: Local Disk (C:)/Games/Toribash-x.xx/screenshots.
    • Steam version: Local Disk (C:)/Program Files(x86)/Steam/steamapps/common/Toribash/screenshots.

Uploading screenshots:

  • Please note if you are reporting something, do NOT use the toribash forum's built in image uploader. It will make it difficult to read and you will most likely be asked to upload it to an image hosting site.
    Instead, upload your screenshots to an image hosting site such as:
    • imgur (much recommended!)
    • Gyazo
    • puush
    • tinypic

Posting the screenshot on the forums: After you have uploaded your screenshot to an image hosting website, you can copy the image's link (right click and copy image address works best) and paste the link in between [img][/img] tags.
Example: [img]https://i.imgur.com/ufAokpx.png[/img].
